Feast of Passover

Passover is an eight-day holiday commemorating the Israelites' emancipation from slavery in ancient Egypt. In the first Passover, a lamb was slain, and Jesus the Messiah in the New Testament, was slain during Passover. After three days GOD raised him from the dead. Christians believe that Jesus the Messiah is their Passover lamb sacrificed for them(1 Corinthians 5:7).

Passover (Hebrew: Pesach)  

Pentecost (Shavuot)

Shavuot is a two-day holiday commemorating GOD giving the Torah (GOD's Law) to the Israelites at Mount Sinai over three thousand years ago. Shavuot, meaning "weeks," is named after counting forty-nine days (seven 'weeks') from the second day of Passover. The fiftieth day after the first day of Passover is also the day when GOD poured out His holy spirit on about one hundred and twenty disciples of Jesus the Messiah (Acts Chapter 2).

Pentecost (Hebrew: Shavuot)AKA Feast of Weeks

Feast of Booths (Sukkot)

Sukkot is a week-long holiday commemorating the forty years the Israelites spent in the desert after escaping slavery in Egypt. Present-day Jews live, eat, and sleep in booths/tents during this holiday week, reminiscing about their time in tents in the desert. Sukkot is likely the true date of Jesus' birth, since "God's word was tabernacled (tented) in the flesh" in Jesus (John 1:14).

Feast of Booths (Hebrew: Sukkot)AKA Feast of Tabernacles
